About

Hi, I'm Ariel! I'm currently an undergraduate studying Computer Science and Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. On campus I'm involved with the MIT Media Lab, Design for America, Camp Kesem, sMITe, and Kappa Alpha Theta. I'm originally from New York City, where I attended Stuyvesant High School. Projects

  • Brain Power
  • Marginalia
  • Machine Learning

Brain Power builds software to transform wearables into neuro-assistive devices for those challenged by autism. I externed as a project manager with Brain Power, leading a team of six developers creating an application across Android mobile and Google Glass platforms.

Marginalia is a Chrome extension that streamlines web page saving and collaborative annotation. Specific sections of text can be highlighted or commented on. The extension also provides space for general notes on the article as a whole. See this video for a demonstration.

I performed research in machine learning with the Center for Computational Learning Systems @ Columbia University. I presented my work at the International Conference on Machine Learning & Applications and the New York Academy of Sciences Machine Learning Symposium.
